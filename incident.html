File 01 — The Chedda Incident

Somewhere between midnight hunger and poor judgment, Greg finds a foil pouch stamped in angry red letters: PROJECT MF CHEDDA — DO NOT CONSUME. Bigfoot tells him to chill. Greg lights a joint instead. The microwave hums like it’s remembering the future. The air thickens. The cheese glows.

Reality kinks sideways. A spiral of molten cheddar punches through the cabin ceiling, dragging ash, crumbs, and loose thoughts into orbit. Greg swears he can hear the universe buffering.

File 02 — The Pull

The cheese doesn’t melt — it detonates softly, like a star folding in on itself. The cabin forgets how to be solid. Greg and Bigfoot are lifted, laughing, screaming, praying — it’s all the same sound now.

Time stretches into orange tunnels. Clocks spin out of their frames. Nacho shrapnel floats like tiny satellites. Bigfoot rides the pull with a nacho still in his hand. Greg tumbles through the vortex in slow motion, eyes wide, reaching for his spinning joint. No matter how hard reality spins, it never leaves his sight.

File 03 — The Tin Hat Protocol

Greg’s emergency plan is simple: when in doubt, add more foil. He upgrades his tin hat mid-teleport, crimping layers like he’s speedrunning a DIY tutorial for quantum paranoia.

The hat doesn’t block the signal — it tunes it. Suddenly Greg can see probability trails, echo timelines, and what Bigfoot would look like if he’d said no to the nachos. He doesn’t like that version, so he leans harder into this one.

File 04 — Spore Prophecy

The landing isn’t really a landing — more like getting gently misfiled into a different forest. The mushrooms here are big enough to file taxes. Their caps glow with symbols that weren’t in any of Greg’s conspiracy folders.

One spore cloud forms a repeating message: twist the dial clockwise to forget, counterclockwise to feed the portal. Greg stares at it for a long time. Bigfoot twists nothing, remembers everything, and keeps walking.

File 05 — The Glitch Return

Eventually, the universe gets tired of being inside out and snaps them back toward something that looks like a cabin again. The pizza is different. The TV is different. The timeline swears nothing has changed.

Greg’s reflection on the screen blinks a few frames late. Bigfoot’s nachos refill themselves whenever no one is looking. Somewhere in the background, the portal pretends to be a question mark.

Meet the Usual Suspects

Greg — Subject: The Paranoid Optimist

Greg didn’t mean to become a multiverse node. He just followed too many late-night threads, smoked too many “this one’s different” joints, and connected one piece of government cheese that probably shouldn’t have existed. He went hunting for proof of Bigfoot and accidentally filmed his own origin story instead.

He’s paranoid, sure — but he’s not wrong nearly as often as everyone would like. Greg connects dots no one else even sees… usually while incredibly baked, with his joint welded to his mouth like a cursor.

Bigfoot — Subject: The Chill

Eight feet of vibes. Snack-based belief system. Camera shy, not clout shy. Bigfoot has been dodging phone cameras and hunters since before the internet, mostly because he refuses to get tagged in anything.

He runs on a strict diet of smoke, snacks, and not caring what dimension he’s currently in as long as there’s food. When Greg showed up trying to “get evidence,” Bigfoot let him stay — mostly because he kept bringing better nachos.

7B_Ω — Subject: The Intern

Officially: a background process assigned to monitor cheddar-based anomalies. Unofficially: the overworked cosmic intern who keeps stamping “do not consume” on things nobody listens to.

7B_Ω is the one who filed this entire mess under “educational material” instead of “containment breach,” which is how you’re reading it now. Whether that was an accident or part of the protocol is still redacted.
