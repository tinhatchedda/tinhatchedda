File 01 — The Chedda Incident

Somewhere between midnight hunger and poor judgment, Greg finds a foil pouch stamped in red: PROJECT MF CHEDDA — DO NOT CONSUME. Bigfoot tells him to chill. Greg lights a joint instead. The microwave hums like it’s remembering the future. The air thickens.

The cheese doesn’t melt — it detonates softly, like a star folding in on itself. The cabin forgets how to be solid. Greg and Bigfoot are lifted, laughing, screaming, praying — it’s all the same sound now. Somewhere in the echo, reality asks: “You sure you’re ready for this?”

File 02 — The Pull

The vortex drags them through a tunnel of clocks, snack wrappers, and abandoned TV signals. Gravity gives up. Greg hangs in the air, spinning slowly, eyes locked wide — the joint still fixed in the corner of his mouth like it’s part of his face now.

Bigfoot drifts beside him, chewing nachos in slow motion, crumbs hovering like tiny satellites. Every crunch echoes down the tunnel like a cosmic countdown. They don’t know where they’re headed, but the portal definitely does.

File 03 — The Tin Hat Protocol

They land in a clearing that looks glitched out of a conspiracy message board: monolithic shrooms, broken clocks, pyramids, and an aurora that smells faintly like burnt cheese. Greg’s homemade tinfoil hat starts buzzing — not metaphorically. Literally humming.

The hat locks onto some off-grid broadcast. Symbols, stock tips, and doomsday memes flood his brain at once. Bigfoot shrugs, adjusts his own foil cap, and decides that if the universe wants to talk, it better bring snacks.

File 04 — Spore Prophecy

In the heart of the shroom field, a towering cap lights up like a projector. A face blooms across the gills, speaking in riddles about memory, markets, and the mouth of the portal. Time drips sideways.

A ring of spores forms around Greg’s feet, spinning like a luminous dial. The voice gives him one instruction, burned into the log in bright neon: Clockwise to forget. Counter-clockwise to feed the portal.

File 05 — The Glitch Return

Reality snaps hard. Greg and Bigfoot slam back into the cabin — same table, same nachos, same question-mark vortex chewing through the ceiling. Only now there’s new clutter: extra timelines stacked in the corners, duplicate snack bags, and a TV showing a version of Greg who never looked away.

The log cuts here, but the feed doesn’t. Somewhere in the static, you can hear them arguing over whether this is the first time it’s happened… or just the only time they remember.

Meet the Usual Suspects

Greg — Subject: The Paranoid Optimist

Web3 degen, chronic over-thinker, and walking red-string corkboard. Greg went hunting for proof of Bigfoot and accidentally found the bug report for reality instead. His tinfoil hat doubles as a multiverse antenna, a market sentiment scanner, and a terrible fashion choice.

He connects dots no one else sees, usually while extremely baked. Half of his theories are wrong, a third are cursed, and the rest are classified. He’s not always right — just right way more often than anyone’s comfortable admitting.

Bigfoot — Subject: The Chill

Eight feet of vibes. Snack-based belief system. Camera shy, not clout shy. Bigfoot has been dodging phone cameras and hunters since dial-up, not because he’s scared — he just refuses to get clipped at a bad angle.

He runs on smoke, nachos, and not giving a single care what dimension he’s technically in as long as the food hits. When Greg showed up trying to “get evidence,” Bigfoot let him stay mostly because he kept bringing better snacks.

7B — Subject: The Intern

Anomaly catalog intern from somewhere between Zeta-7 and HR. Official duties: label the weirdness, file the glitches, and absolutely do not interfere. Unofficial duties: interfere constantly, leak files, and stan Greg & Bigfoot like a cosmic reality show.

7B is the one who routed these logs to your device. If you’re reading this, either you were meant to be… or somebody in the archive is definitely getting written up.
