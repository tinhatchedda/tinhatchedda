File 01 — The Chedda Incident

Somewhere between midnight hunger and poor judgment, Greg finds a foil pouch stamped in angry red letters: PROJECT MF CHEDDA — DO NOT CONSUME. Bigfoot tells him to chill. Greg lights a joint instead. The microwave hums like it’s remembering the future. The air thickens. The cheese glows.

Reality kinks sideways. A spiral of molten cheddar punches through the cabin ceiling, dragging ash, crumbs, and loose thoughts into orbit.

File 02 — The Pull

The cheese doesn’t melt — it detonates softly, like a star folding in on itself. Greg feels his shoes forget gravity. Bigfoot’s chair forgets the floor. Nacho shrapnel hangs in the air like tiny, crunchy satellites.

Greg drifts backward toward the vortex, joint still clamped between his lips, eyes wide but weirdly impressed. Bigfoot floats with him, unfazed, still chewing. Clocks, cans, and VHS tapes spiral around them as the cabin unzips into pure swirl.

Somewhere in the echo, reality quietly asks: “You sure you’re ready for this?” Greg doesn’t answer. He just inhales.

File 03 — The Tin Hat Protocol

On the other side, the sky looks like a late-night cartoon had a panic attack on a black hole. Mushrooms watch the watchers. Pyramids hum like Wi-Fi routers for ghosts. Every billboard is targeted directly at Greg’s anxiety.

Greg finally does what any responsible paranoiac would do: tightens his tin foil hat, bumps his threat level from “vibing” to “trust no feed,” and starts treating every glitch as a message. Bigfoot treats every glitch as a snack opportunity.

File 04 — Spore Prophecy

Deep in the spore fields, a towering mushroom priest broadcasts a message straight into Greg’s skull: the Chedda vortex is a key, not a glitch.

Clockwise to forget. Memories dissolve, timelines reset, clones spawn with slightly worse taste in snacks. Counter-clockwise to feed the portal. The vortex grows, chewing through fake timelines and knockoff realities until only the original signal remains.

Greg writes all of this down on a napkin. Bigfoot uses the napkin as a plate.

File 05 — The Glitch Return

Back in the cabin, nothing quite matches the save file. The TV runs scenes that never aired. The microwave door opens on static. Greg catches his own reflection lagging by half a frame.

Bigfoot shrugs it off. Greg does not. Somewhere between the skipped frames, something else blinks — watching them both, waiting for the next rewind.
