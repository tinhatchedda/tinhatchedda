THE CHEDDA FILES – VOL. 1: THE INCIDENT LOGS

These recordings were never supposed to exist. The government labeled the event “Project MF Chedda” and buried the footage under six layers of clearance and one very confused intern.

But the signal leaks—through static, through memes, through the kind of Wi‑Fi that smells like burnt popcorn and paranoia.

What you’re about to see are the recovered fragments: five visual glitches left behind when Greg and Bigfoot cracked open a portal with a joint, a microwave, and the world’s most dangerous cheese.

View at your own risk. Side effects may include hunger, time dilation, and an uncomfortable awareness that you’ve been here before.

Welcome to Tin Hat Chedda. Reality melts at 420 °F.

FILE 01 — THE CHEDDA INCIDENT

Somewhere between midnight hunger and poor judgment, Greg finds a foil pouch stamped in red:

PROJECT MF CHEDDA — DO NOT CONSUME.

Bigfoot tells him to chill. Greg lights a joint instead.

The microwave hums like it’s remembering the future.

The air thickens. The cheese glows.

For one perfect, terrifying moment, Greg swears the universe winks at him.

Then everything blinks back.

FILE 02 — THE PULL

The cheese doesn’t melt—it detonates softly, like a star folding in on itself.

Golden liquid stretches the walls until the cabin forgets how to be solid.

Greg and Bigfoot are lifted, weightless, laughing, screaming, praying—it’s all the same sound now.

The joint spins in slow motion, a burning comet in a storm of nacho light.

Somewhere in the echo, reality asks, “You sure you’re ready for this?”

FILE 03 — CLOCKLAND ARRIVAL

They hit the ground in silence that ticks.

Mushrooms breathe. Clocks bloom from the soil, swinging like pendulums lost in a dream.

Every time Greg blinks, the sky rewrites itself.

Bigfoot exhales a perfect smoke ring—it freezes midair, turns into a halo, then quietly orbits his head.

“Bro,” he says, “I think time got high before us.” And he’s probably right.

FILE 04 — PROPHECY OF THE SHROOM

A towering mushroom unfolds, glowing from within, each pulse syncing with their heartbeats.

The spores rise like pixels, rearranging into words they can’t unread:

“Clockwise to forget… counterclockwise to feed the portal.”

Greg’s eyes widen. Bigfoot lights another.

The forest bends around them, reality twitching like bad reception.

They’ve just been given instructions—and neither one knows if they already followed them.

FILE 05 — THE GLITCH RETURN

The cabin snaps back into existence like a bad magic trick.

Everything’s where it was—almost.

The cheese hovers midair, humming a frequency that feels like déjà vu.

The TV plays static shaped like memories.

Greg stares at his reflection; it waves first.

Bigfoot sighs, takes a drag, and mutters, “Guess we’re home?”

The hum deepens. The universe smiles. Round two.

END TRANSMISSION — VOL. 1

The feed burns out mid‑sentence.

Greg swears it winked back.

Nobody’s sure if they made it home or just respawned in another rerun.

If you can still hear the hum, congratulations—you’re already part of the experiment.
