DOSSIER 01 — CONTEXT

The Chedda Files – Vol. 1: The Incident Logs

These recordings were never supposed to exist. The government labeled the event “Project MF Chedda” and buried the footage under six layers of clearance, three NDAs, and one very confused intern.

But leaks happen. And when your test subject is an eight-foot stoner cryptid and his paranoid tin-hatted best friend, the signal doesn’t stay buried. It slips out through static, memes, and Wi-Fi that smells like burnt popcorn and bad decisions.

What you’re about to see are the recovered fragments: five visual glitches that survived when Greg and Bigfoot accidentally microwaved a block of government cheese and tore a hole in their living room.

View at your own risk. Side effects may include hunger, time dilation, and the uncomfortable realization that you’ve definitely been here before.

Welcome to Tin Hat Chedda™. Reality melts at 420 °F.

FILE 01

The Chedda Incident

“Just heat it up, what’s the worst that could happen?”

Somewhere between midnight hunger and poor judgment, Greg finds a foil pouch stamped in angry red letters: PROJECT MF CHEDDA — DO NOT CONSUME.

Bigfoot squints, shrugs, and tells him to relax. Greg sparks a joint instead of reading the fine print. The microwave hums like it’s remembering the future. The air thickens. The cheese starts to glow.

When the timer dings, everything dings. Gravity twitches. The TV glitches. The nachos boil into a swirling question mark that looks suspiciously like a portal.

FILE 02

The Pull

Gravity called in sick.

The cheese doesn’t melt — it detonates softly, like a star folding in on itself. The cabin forgets how to be inside. The living room stretches into a spiral of clocks, cans, and stoner regret.

Greg and Bigfoot are lifted out of their seats, drifting weightless toward the vortex. Greg clutches his joint like a life-raft. Bigfoot refuses to drop his nachos.

For a split second, Greg swears the vortex winks at him. Somewhere in the echo, reality asks: “You sure you’re ready for this?”

FILE 03

Clockland

Time is high and you’re late to everything.

They land in a field where every step crunches on broken clock faces. Time is scattered like cheap glass. Mushrooms the size of UFOs glow with impossible colors, eyes blinking in slow-motion.

Greg checks his phone — no service, 4,206 missed notifications. Bigfoot doesn’t care. He’s already investigating a cosmic shroom that looks like it wants to negotiate.

The sky overhead loops on itself, galaxies spinning like vinyl. Somewhere in the static, a voice repeats: “Remember why you came here, Greg.”

FILE 04

The Shroom Prophecy

Twist clockwise to forget. Counterclockwise to feed the rabbit hole.

A colossal mushroom wakes up, eyes opening like stained glass windows. Its halo spills golden spores that swirl into glowing text around Greg and Bigfoot.

The words orbit them: “TWIST CLOCKWISE TO FORGET” and “COUNTERCLOCKWISE TO FEED THE RABBIT HOLE.”

Bigfoot, obviously, twists counterclockwise. The spores laugh. Somewhere, a wallet address pings.

FILE 05

The Glitch Return

Home, but not quite.

Reality snaps back like a rubber band. Greg and Bigfoot are suddenly at the table again. The cheese is just… cheese. The TV is off. Everything is quiet.

Then Greg notices the TV reflection: a version of himself still floating in Clockland, eyes wide, buffering. Bigfoot’s reflection winks, even though he isn’t moving.

The microwave display quietly reads: “VOL. 2 READY.”

Meet the Usual Suspects

GREG — The Tin Hat

Paranoid stoner. Surprisingly sharp. Accidentally opened a portal with snacks.

Greg’s the guy who double-encrypts his memes, won’t use public Wi-Fi, and still somehow ends up in every government file. He’s not a scientist — he just doomscrolled enough late-night forums to know that Bigfoot was real and probably blazing in the woods nearby.

Armed with a tinfoil hat, a half-charged phone, and a chronic supply, he set out to “catch proof” of Bigfoot. He forgot to hit record, made a friend instead, and now he’s stuck co-owning an interdimensional nacho incident.

BIGFOOT — The Chill

Eight feet of snack-powered serenity.

Bigfoot has been dodging cameras for decades. Not because he’s shy — he just hates bad angles. He’s the universe’s most laid-back cryptid: always hungry, always smoking, and permanently down for “one more.”

He never meant to be part of a classified project; he just followed the smell of nachos into the wrong cabin. Now he’s stuck in a government cheese conspiracy with a paranoid human who won’t stop narrating everything like a podcast.
